Nisab, Threshold & Rulings

Nisab is the minimum zakatable wealth threshold. This calculator uses 87.48 grams of gold and 612.36 grams of silver. If net zakatable wealth is at or above the selected threshold for one lunar year, zakat is calculated at 2.5%.

Gold nisab:
87.48g × 24K gold value × 83% resale setting.
Silver nisab:
612.36g × silver value × 83% resale setting.
Zakat rate:
2.5% of net zakatable wealth after deductible liabilities.

General fatwa-style notes for users

  • This tool is an estimate, not a personal fatwa. For edge cases, ask a qualified mufti or local scholar.
  • Zakat is normally due on cash, gold, silver, trade goods, business inventory, strong receivables, and investment assets held for a lunar year.
  • Primary residence, personal vehicle, and ordinary personal-use items are generally not zakatable unless held for trade.
  • Some scholars deduct only immediate/short-term liabilities, while long-term debts may be treated differently.
  • Gold jewellery rulings differ by school and local scholarship. This calculator includes gold jewellery by default; adjust according to the ruling you follow.
Transparency: The gold/silver values are editable because live scraping from BAJUS may fail in a browser. Update the market rates from BAJUS, keep resale percentage at 83% if you follow that local estimate, then calculate.
